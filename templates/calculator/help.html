Quick Start Guide

Enter the following building dimensions:

  • Height: Total height of the building from ground level
  • In-wind Depth: Depth of the building in the direction of wind
  • Width: Width of the building perpendicular to wind direction
All dimensions should be entered in meters (m).

Enter the following site parameters:

  • Site Altitude: Altitude of the building site above sea level
  • Terrain Category: Category of terrain surrounding the building (1-4)
  • Upwind Slope: Slope of the terrain upwind of the building
  • Orographic Factor: Factor accounting for local topography effects
Terrain categories are defined in the standards:
  • Category 1: Open sea, lakes, flat terrain
  • Category 2: Open country with low vegetation
  • Category 3: Suburban areas, industrial areas
  • Category 4: Urban areas with tall buildings

Specify the following opening parameters:

  • Windward Openings: Number of openings on windward face
  • Leeward Openings: Number of openings on leeward face
  • Parallel Openings: Number of openings on parallel faces
  • Windward Area: Total area of windward openings (mm²)
  • Leeward Area: Total area of leeward openings (mm²)
  • Parallel Area: Total area of parallel openings (mm²)
Opening areas should be entered in square millimeters (mm²).

Before calculating:

  • Review all input parameters for accuracy
  • Ensure all required fields are filled
  • Check that values are within reasonable ranges

Click the "Calculate" button to perform the calculations.

The calculation process may take a few seconds. Please wait for the results to be displayed.

Frequently Asked Questions

The calculator supports the following standards:
  • Eurocode 1 (EN 1991-1-4)
  • ASCE 7
  • AS/NZS 1170.2
You can select the appropriate standard for your region in the calculator settings.

The calculator provides accurate results based on the input parameters and selected standard. However, the accuracy depends on:
  • Accuracy of input parameters
  • Appropriateness of the selected standard
  • Complexity of the building geometry
For complex buildings or critical applications, we recommend verifying the results with a qualified structural engineer.
