About the Wind Load Calculator

The Wind Load Calculator is a comprehensive tool designed to help structural engineers, architects, and building designers calculate wind loads on buildings according to international standards.

Methodology

Calculation Process

The calculator follows a systematic approach to determine wind loads on buildings:

  1. Basic Wind Velocity

    Calculate the basic wind velocity considering directional and seasonal factors.

  2. Terrain Parameters

    Determine terrain parameters based on the surrounding environment.

  3. Reference Height

    Calculate the reference height based on building dimensions.

  4. Exposure Factor

    Compute the exposure factor considering terrain roughness and height.

  5. Peak Velocity Pressure

    Calculate the peak velocity pressure using the basic velocity pressure and exposure factor.

  6. Pressure Coefficients

    Determine pressure coefficients for different building zones.

  7. Net Pressure

    Calculate the net pressure on each zone considering internal and external pressures.

Supported Standards

Eurocode 1
EN 1991-1-4

Actions on structures - Part 1-4: General actions - Wind actions. Provides guidance for calculating wind loads on buildings in Europe.

ASCE 7
Minimum Design Loads

Minimum Design Loads and Associated Criteria for Buildings and Other Structures. Standard for wind load calculations in the United States.

AS/NZS 1170.2
Wind Actions

Structural design actions - Wind actions. Standard for wind load calculations in Australia and New Zealand.

Assumptions and Limitations

Key Assumptions
  • The building is assumed to be rigid and not subject to dynamic effects.
  • Wind flow is assumed to be steady and uniform.
  • Terrain is assumed to be homogeneous upwind of the building.
  • Internal pressure is assumed to be uniform throughout the building.
Limitations
  • The calculator is not suitable for buildings with complex geometries or significant dynamic effects.
  • Local wind effects such as funnelling or channeling are not considered.
  • The calculator does not account for wind-induced vibrations or aeroelastic effects.
  • Results should be verified by a qualified structural engineer.
